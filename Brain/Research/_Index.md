# Research — Index

**Durable research findings.** Knowledge that cost real effort to establish (measured, sourced, or learned the hard way) and that **must not be re-researched** on the next project.

This tier exists because research keeps dying inside the project folder that produced it. A finding here is *portable*: it was true before the project that produced it and stays true after. Project-specific *decisions* live in `Projects/` and `Decisions/`; the transferable **why** and **how** live here.

## Canonical rule

> **One finding, one home.** Cite and link from a project note — never restate a finding there, or the two will drift.

When a project produces a reusable finding, it goes **here** and the project note links to it. Project notes keep only what is specific to that project (the choices made, not the evidence base).

## Staleness

Facts about moving targets decay. Anything version- or support-dependent (platform support, pricing, model costs) carries its **verification date inline** — treat a fact more than ~6 months past its date as unverified and re-check before relying on it. Craft findings (measurements, method, theory) do not decay and need no re-check.
